# Common Laboratory Test Reference Ranges

## Complete Blood Count (CBC)

### Hemoglobin (Hb)
- **Male**: 13.5 - 17.5 g/dL
- **Female**: 12.0 - 16.0 g/dL
- **Low**: Anemia — may indicate iron deficiency, chronic disease, blood loss, or bone marrow problems.
- **High**: Polycythemia — may indicate dehydration, lung disease, or bone marrow disorders.

### White Blood Cell Count (WBC)
- **Normal Range**: 4,500 - 11,000 cells/μL
- **Low (Leukopenia)**: May indicate bone marrow problems, autoimmune disorders, or certain infections.
- **High (Leukocytosis)**: May indicate infection, inflammation, stress, or leukemia.

### Platelet Count
- **Normal Range**: 150,000 - 400,000 cells/μL
- **Low (Thrombocytopenia)**: May indicate bone marrow problems, immune disorders, or medications.
- **High (Thrombocytosis)**: May indicate inflammation, infection, or bone marrow disorders.

### Red Blood Cell Count (RBC)
- **Male**: 4.7 - 6.1 million cells/μL
- **Female**: 4.2 - 5.4 million cells/μL
- **Low**: Anemia or blood loss.
- **High**: Dehydration, lung disease, or kidney tumors.

### Hematocrit (HCT)
- **Male**: 38.3% - 48.6%
- **Female**: 35.5% - 44.9%
- **Reflects the proportion of blood volume occupied by red blood cells.**

## Liver Function Tests (LFT)

### ALT (Alanine Aminotransferase)
- **Normal Range**: 7 - 56 U/L
- **High**: Liver damage, hepatitis, or fatty liver disease.

### AST (Aspartate Aminotransferase)
- **Normal Range**: 10 - 40 U/L
- **High**: Liver damage, muscle damage, or heart problems.

### ALP (Alkaline Phosphatase)
- **Normal Range**: 44 - 147 U/L
- **High**: Liver disease, bile duct obstruction, or bone disorders.

### Bilirubin (Total)
- **Normal Range**: 0.1 - 1.2 mg/dL
- **High**: Liver disease, bile duct problems, or hemolytic anemia (jaundice).

### Albumin
- **Normal Range**: 3.5 - 5.0 g/dL
- **Low**: Liver disease, kidney disease, or malnutrition.

---

## Kidney Function Tests (KFT / RFT)

### Creatinine
- **Male**: 0.74 - 1.35 mg/dL
- **Female**: 0.59 - 1.04 mg/dL
- **High**: Kidney dysfunction, dehydration, or muscle damage.

### Blood Urea Nitrogen (BUN)
- **Normal Range**: 7 - 20 mg/dL
- **High**: Kidney disease, dehydration, high-protein diet, or heart failure.

### eGFR (Estimated Glomerular Filtration Rate)
- **Normal**: >90 mL/min/1.73m²
- **Mild decrease**: 60-89 mL/min/1.73m²
- **Moderate decrease**: 30-59 mL/min/1.73m²
- **Severe decrease**: 15-29 mL/min/1.73m²
- **Kidney failure**: <15 mL/min/1.73m²

### Uric Acid
- **Male**: 3.4 - 7.0 mg/dL
- **Female**: 2.4 - 6.0 mg/dL
- **High**: Gout, kidney disease, or high-purine diet.

---

## Lipid Profile

### Total Cholesterol
- **Desirable**: <200 mg/dL
- **Borderline High**: 200-239 mg/dL
- **High**: ≥240 mg/dL

### LDL Cholesterol (Bad Cholesterol)
- **Optimal**: <100 mg/dL
- **Near Optimal**: 100-129 mg/dL
- **Borderline High**: 130-159 mg/dL
- **High**: 160-189 mg/dL
- **Very High**: ≥190 mg/dL

### HDL Cholesterol (Good Cholesterol)
- **Low (Risk)**: <40 mg/dL (men), <50 mg/dL (women)
- **Desirable**: ≥60 mg/dL
- **Higher HDL is protective against heart disease.**

### Triglycerides
- **Normal**: <150 mg/dL
- **Borderline High**: 150-199 mg/dL
- **High**: 200-499 mg/dL
- **Very High**: ≥500 mg/dL

---

## Diabetes Markers

### Fasting Blood Sugar (FBS)
- **Normal**: 70-99 mg/dL
- **Pre-diabetes**: 100-125 mg/dL
- **Diabetes**: ≥126 mg/dL

### HbA1c (Glycated Hemoglobin)
- **Normal**: <5.7%
- **Pre-diabetes**: 5.7% - 6.4%
- **Diabetes**: ≥6.5%
- **Reflects average blood sugar over the past 2-3 months.**

### Post-Prandial Blood Sugar (PPBS)
- **Normal**: <140 mg/dL
- **Pre-diabetes**: 140-199 mg/dL
- **Diabetes**: ≥200 mg/dL

---

## Thyroid Function Tests

### TSH (Thyroid Stimulating Hormone)
- **Normal Range**: 0.4 - 4.0 mIU/L
- **High**: Hypothyroidism (underactive thyroid)
- **Low**: Hyperthyroidism (overactive thyroid)

### Free T4 (Thyroxine)
- **Normal Range**: 0.8 - 1.8 ng/dL
- **High**: Hyperthyroidism
- **Low**: Hypothyroidism

### Free T3 (Triiodothyronine)
- **Normal Range**: 2.3 - 4.2 pg/mL
- **Used alongside TSH and T4 for comprehensive thyroid assessment.**

---

## Electrolytes

### Sodium (Na+)
- **Normal Range**: 136 - 145 mEq/L
- **Low (Hyponatremia)**: Fluid overload, kidney problems, or certain medications.
- **High (Hypernatremia)**: Dehydration or excessive salt intake.

### Potassium (K+)
- **Normal Range**: 3.5 - 5.0 mEq/L
- **Low (Hypokalemia)**: May cause muscle weakness, cramping.
- **High (Hyperkalemia)**: May cause heart rhythm problems.

### Calcium (Ca2+)
- **Normal Range**: 8.5 - 10.5 mg/dL
- **Low**: Vitamin D deficiency, parathyroid problems.
- **High**: Hyperparathyroidism, cancer, or excessive vitamin D.

---

## Iron Studies

### Serum Iron
- **Male**: 65 - 175 μg/dL
- **Female**: 50 - 170 μg/dL

### Ferritin
- **Male**: 20 - 500 ng/mL
- **Female**: 20 - 200 ng/mL
- **Low**: Iron deficiency anemia.
- **High**: Iron overload, inflammation, or liver disease.

### TIBC (Total Iron Binding Capacity)
- **Normal Range**: 250 - 370 μg/dL
- **High**: Iron deficiency.
- **Low**: Iron overload or chronic disease.

---

## Inflammatory Markers

### CRP (C-Reactive Protein)
- **Normal**: <1.0 mg/L (low risk)
- **Moderate Risk**: 1.0 - 3.0 mg/L
- **High Risk**: >3.0 mg/L
- **Indicates inflammation in the body.**

### ESR (Erythrocyte Sedimentation Rate)
- **Male**: 0 - 15 mm/hr
- **Female**: 0 - 20 mm/hr
- **High**: Inflammation, infection, or autoimmune disease.

---

## Vitamins

### Vitamin D (25-Hydroxy)
- **Deficient**: <20 ng/mL
- **Insufficient**: 20-29 ng/mL
- **Sufficient**: 30-100 ng/mL
- **Toxicity**: >100 ng/mL

### Vitamin B12
- **Normal Range**: 200 - 900 pg/mL
- **Low**: May cause fatigue, weakness, neurological problems.
