# Common Medical Terminology Glossary

## Blood-Related Terms

- **Anemia**: A condition where you don't have enough healthy red blood cells to carry adequate oxygen to your body's tissues.
- **Hemoglobin**: The protein in red blood cells that carries oxygen throughout the body.
- **Hematocrit**: The percentage of your blood that is made up of red blood cells.
- **Leukocytes**: White blood cells that fight infection.
- **Thrombocytes / Platelets**: Small blood cells that help your body form clots to stop bleeding.
- **Hemolysis**: The destruction of red blood cells.
- **Polycythemia**: A condition where the body makes too many red blood cells.
- **Leukocytosis**: An elevated white blood cell count, often indicating infection or inflammation.
- **Leukopenia**: An abnormally low white blood cell count.
- **Thrombocytopenia**: Low platelet count that may cause easy bruising or bleeding.

## Liver-Related Terms

- **Hepatitis**: Inflammation of the liver, often caused by viral infections.
- **Jaundice**: Yellowing of the skin and eyes caused by excess bilirubin.
- **Cirrhosis**: Chronic liver damage leading to scarring and liver failure.
- **Fatty Liver Disease (Steatosis)**: Accumulation of fat in liver cells.
- **Transaminases (ALT/AST)**: Enzymes released when liver cells are damaged.

## Kidney-Related Terms

- **Creatinine**: A waste product from normal muscle metabolism, filtered by the kidneys.
- **GFR (Glomerular Filtration Rate)**: A measure of how well your kidneys filter waste.
- **Proteinuria**: Excess protein in the urine, may indicate kidney damage.
- **Nephropathy**: Kidney disease or damage.
- **Dialysis**: A treatment that filters waste from your blood when kidneys can't.

## Heart-Related Terms

- **Arrhythmia**: An irregular heartbeat.
- **Atherosclerosis**: Build-up of plaque in artery walls.
- **Hypertension**: High blood pressure.
- **Hypotension**: Low blood pressure.
- **Myocardial Infarction (MI)**: Heart attack.
- **Angina**: Chest pain caused by reduced blood flow to the heart.

## Diabetes-Related Terms

- **HbA1c**: A measure of average blood sugar over the past 2-3 months.
- **Hyperglycemia**: High blood sugar.
- **Hypoglycemia**: Low blood sugar.
- **Insulin Resistance**: When cells don't respond well to insulin.
- **Type 1 Diabetes**: Autoimmune condition where the body attacks insulin-producing cells.
- **Type 2 Diabetes**: Condition where the body doesn't use insulin properly.

## Thyroid-Related Terms

- **Hypothyroidism**: Underactive thyroid — body makes too little thyroid hormone.
- **Hyperthyroidism**: Overactive thyroid — body makes too much thyroid hormone.
- **TSH**: Thyroid Stimulating Hormone — controls thyroid function.
- **Goiter**: Enlargement of the thyroid gland.

## General Medical Terms

- **Acute**: Sudden onset, usually severe, and short duration.
- **Chronic**: Long-lasting or frequently recurring condition.
- **Benign**: Not harmful or cancerous.
- **Malignant**: Cancerous and potentially life-threatening.
- **Inflammation**: The body's immune response to injury or infection.
- **Edema**: Swelling caused by excess fluid trapped in body tissues.
- **Biopsy**: Removal of tissue for examination.
- **Prognosis**: The likely course of a disease or condition.
- **Idiopathic**: Of unknown cause.
- **Asymptomatic**: Having no symptoms.

## Radiology Terms

- **CT Scan (Computed Tomography)**: X-ray images combined by computer to create cross-sectional views.
- **MRI (Magnetic Resonance Imaging)**: Uses magnetic fields and radio waves for detailed body images.
- **Ultrasound**: Uses sound waves to create images of organs.
- **X-ray / Radiograph**: Uses radiation to create images of bones and some organs.
- **Contrast Agent**: A substance used to enhance visibility of structures in imaging.
- **Lesion**: An area of abnormal tissue, which may or may not be cancerous.
- **Opacity**: An area that appears white on an X-ray, may indicate fluid, tumor, or inflammation.
- **Effusion**: Abnormal collection of fluid in a body cavity.
